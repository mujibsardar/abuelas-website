Frequently Asked Questions

Is the app available on Android?

Currently, the app is only available on the Apple App Store for iOS devices. An Android version is coming soon—stay tuned for updates!

How do I place an order?

Browse local meals, select your favorite, and choose either pickup or delivery. When you order, you pay the cook directly using their preferred payment method.

Where can I legally operate a MEHKO in California?

You can legally operate a Microenterprise Home Kitchen Operation (MEHKO) only in California counties that have opted into the program. As of September 2025, these counties include: Alameda, Amador, Contra Costa, Imperial, Lake, Los Angeles (excluding Pasadena, Long Beach, and Vernon), Monterey, Riverside, San Benito, San Diego, San Mateo, Santa Barbara, Santa Clara, Santa Cruz, Sierra, Solano, and Sonoma. The City of Berkeley has also opted in. Be sure to check with your local county health department or visit mehko.org for the most current list.

What payment methods are accepted?

Payments are made directly to the cook, so you’ll use the method they accept—whether it’s cash, card, or mobile payment.

Is delivery available?

Currently, the cook determines whether they offer delivery, pickup, or both. The cook will provide specific instructions for fulfilling your order.

How can I find meals near me?

Use our radius search feature to explore meals available within your chosen distance, ensuring you always find options close to home.

Do I need a business license or permit to cook and sell food from my home?

Yes, in California, you’ll need a Microenterprise Home Kitchen Operation (MEHKO) permit to legally cook and sell meals from your home kitchen. This permit ensures compliance with local health and safety regulations.

How do I obtain a MEHKO permit?

You can apply for a MEHKO permit through your county's health department in California. The process usually involves filling out an application, paying a fee, and passing a kitchen inspection. For more detailed information, visit mehko.org. To streamline your application process, use mehko.ai.

How long does it take and how much does it cost to get a MEHKO permit?

The timeline and fees for obtaining a MEHKO permit vary by county. Typically, the application process takes a few weeks, and permit fees usually range between $100 and $500 annually. We recommend contacting your local county health department for exact details or visit mehko.org. For a streamlined application experience, try mehko.ai.

Learn About MEHKO & Home-Based Cooking

Discover essential resources about Microenterprise Home Kitchen Operations (MEHKOs)—a type of small-scale, home-based food business that allows individuals to legally prepare and sell meals from their private residences.
